State of Palestine

Population: 5 millions (Approx.)

Area: 6,220 square kilometers(Approx.)

Currency: The official currency is the Israeli new shekel (ILS) and the Jordanian dinar (JOD)

Historical Dates and Movements:

1. Balfour Declaration (1917): The Balfour Declaration by the British government expressed support for the establishment of a "national home for the Jewish people" in Palestine.

2. Partition Plan (1947): The United Nations proposed a partition plan, leading to the establishment of the State of Israel and triggering conflict.

3. Nakba (1948):The Nakba, or "catastrophe," refers to the displacement of Palestinian Arabs during the 1948 Arab-Israeli war and the establishment of the State of Israel.

4. Six-Day War (1967):Israel occupied the West Bank, East Jerusalem, and the Gaza Strip during the Six-Day War, shaping the ongoing Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

5. Oslo Accords (1993):The Oslo Accords marked a series of agreements between Israel and the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO), aiming to establish a framework for the future relations between the two entities.

6. Second Intifada (2000-2005):A period of intensified Israeli-Palestinian violence, triggered by various factors, including the collapse of peace talks.
